                                                                                                      superjamie 4 years ago

                                                                                                      I agree with your statement that a deep understanding of protocols is required to troubleshoot issues, people always come to me saying "How do I read packet captures?" and I reply "Have you got two days to learn about the OSI model, Ethernet, IP, TCP and UDP, plus how NICs, switches, and routers work?". One needs to understand all these concepts before a packet capture with a duplicate IP or TCP Fast Retransmission becomes understandable.

                                                                                                      However, I think you go about it the wrong way.

                                                                                                      Learning "tcp[13] &2!=0" is fairly specific to tcpdump, and the logical "AND Not Equal To" is opaque to many. Wireshark's "tcp.flags.syn eq 1" is much more intrinsic and approachable. Wireshark also has an arguably superior way of displaying and expanding its protocol dissectors, plus it lets you right click and "Prepare filter". Both of these can enable learning of protocols and filtering of desired information through visual inspection of intrinsic protocol terms, rather than learning random commands full of symbols and numbers you'll never use anywhere else.
